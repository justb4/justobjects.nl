## 1. map5topo - rich topographic map of The Netherlands

According to [map5.nl](https://map5.nl) customers we have a product to be proud of.
I say specifically "we" as [map5topo](https://map5topo.nl) is developed 
together with top Dutch digital cartographer [Niene Boeijen](https://nieneb.nl/online-projects/map5/).
The map5topo project started in April 2022 and is ongoing since.

{{< a-img data-href="https://app.map5.nl/map5topo/full/" data-src="/uploads/2022/map5topo/naarden.jpg" data-caption="map5topo Naarden" >}}

So what is the map5topo map about? 
In short: it is a digital raster+vector map covering The Netherlands 
constructed with Dutch Open Data and deployed via web *"mapping"* services and apps. That is a mouthful, we'll break this up next.
I often say to friends *"...like Google Maps, but prettier and much more detailed"*. 

### Try the demo!

We can spend many words here, but if you are curious, [try the free demo](https://app.map5.nl/map5topo/full/).

{{< a-img data-href="https://app.map5.nl/map5topo/full/" data-src="/uploads/2022/map5topo/demo-full.jpg" data-caption="map5topo demo" >}}

### Constructed with Dutch Open Data
These days, a digital map is created from "source data".

map5topo source data originates from Open Datasets like the Dutch [Key Registries, Basisregistraties](https://www.geobasisregistraties.nl/): BAG, BRT, BGT, BRK, ..., provided
via [Kadaster PDOK](https://pdok.nl) and from [OpenStreetMap](https://openstreetmap.org) data. Our challenge was to combine all these datasets into one
uniform data model. I think we did a nice effort: take "the-best-of" from each dataset, unification in feature classification, plus
scale-based detailing. See the [data design](https://map5topo.nl/design/data/) for details.

{{< a-img data-href="https://map5topo.nl/design/data/" data-src="/uploads/2022/map5topo/data-design.jpg" data-caption="map5topo data design" >}}

### Deployed via web mapping services

map5topo is provided commercially by map5.nl via standardized "tiled" web services like [OGC WMTS](https://www.ogc.org/standards/wmts/), but also "XYZ" 
(Google/OSM tiles, a.k.a. Web Mercator) tiles. Currently, mainly raster (image) tiles, including "HQ Retina" (double density), but also experimental Vector Tiles.

Customers can integrate these maps into their applications. A well-known example is [Wandelnet](https://www.wandelnet.nl/), a major hiking site in The Netherlands.

{{< a-img data-href="https://www.wandelnet.nl/wandelroute/1874/Veluwe-Zwerfpad-etappe-02" data-src="/uploads/2022/map5topo/wandelnet.jpg" data-caption="map5topo wandelnet" >}}

There's also various [apps we provide](https://app.map5.nl/), like the [KadViewer](https://kadviewer.map5.nl/), originally a pilot-viewer for Dutch Kadaster. 

### Try it on your phone!

There is a free map5topo app for mobile devices like smartphones and tablets for both [Android](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=nl.map5topo.viewerapp&hl=en) and Apple IOS like iPhone.
Developed by [Bart Louwers](https://github.com/louwers).

{{< a-img data-href="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=nl.map5topo.viewerapp&hl=en" data-src="/uploads/2022/map5topo/map5topo-app-0.jpg" data-caption="map5topo app" >}}

### Open Source ?

In the planning. [Some repos are already open](https://github.com/map5nl). Let me know if you like to co-develop.

## 2. OpenStreetMap Mapping

I am supposed to be a veteran OSM-mapper, [my profile](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/justb) registered in 2005! 
But for many years my mapping efforts were zero.
But in recent years I picked up mapping again. 
Regular mapping like hiking paths, and specialized projects like Dutch buildings and addresses with the [JOSM BAG Updater](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/NL:BAGupdate).

Mainly mapping in The Netherlands and Spain (latter see below).
Over 17000 contributions in the last year now!

## 4. OpenStreetMap Workshops in Spain

Spain is a huge country. Many rural areas like [The Alpujarras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpujarras) are not mapped in great detail.
Though there are still very active mappers in the area. From the beginning I started adding mainly new hiking paths, surveying with GPS via [CoMaps](https://www.comaps.app/).
I also joined the [Spanish OSM Community](https://openstreetmap.es/) (OSM-ES), simply by joining the OSM-ES Telegram group and weekly video-meetups.

In Spain I learned to follow the [Catastro Buildings and Addresses import](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Spanish_Cadastre/Buildings_import) processes 
and I am working on a possible [SIOSE Landcover/Landuse import](https://github.com/justb4/SIOSE2OSM) (WIP).

My gratitude goes out to [Héctor Ochoa Ortiz](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/Robot8A) who introduced me 
to the welcoming Spanish OSM community and helped along the way.

After having given a mobile OSM mapping workshop at [FOSS4GNL Middelburg 2023](https://osmws.osgeo.nl/) and talking to local people in my village, I got the idea to
organize OpenStreetMap workshops here. We aptly named our group here **"Mapas y Tapas"**. 
The idea being to eventually have Mapping Parties: meet in a bar, map on the streets and "en el campo", reconvene with drinks and the all-abundant "tapas".
But first some education was required. I gave several workshops (see below) to learn mobile mapping with 
[EveryDoor](https://every-door.app/) and [StreetComplete](https://streetcomplete.app/). 
The latest workshops I even provided in Spanish (with some help of local friends)! Also some [CoMaps](https://www.comaps.app/) instruction, 
as people get lost while hiking using Google Maps.

{{< a-img data-href="https://alpumapa.xyz" data-src="/uploads/2022/spain/alpumapa-1.jpg" data-caption="alpumapa 1" >}}

There is one website for these workshops, also for self-study: 

[alpumapa.xyz](https://alpumapa.xyz), or in Spanish at [alpumapa.xyz/es](https://alpumapa.xyz/es/).
